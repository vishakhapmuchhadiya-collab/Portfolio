I started writing not because I was told to, but because I kept noticing the same problem: companies with genuinely useful products buried on page three of Google, behind competitors with thinner content and weaker expertise. That frustrated me enough to do something about it.

Over the past year, I've written more than 30+ pieces of long-form, SEO-optimized content for clients in SaaS, retail automation, and web technology. I've covered everything from accounts receivable automation and deduction management to retail mobile apps and AI-powered data extraction, not as a generalist, but as someone who actually studies the subject before writing a single word.

My approach combines solid keyword research (Ahrefs, SEMrush) with writing that doesn't read like it was assembled from search results. The goal is always the same: content that ranks because it genuinely answers the question better than anyone else.

Beyond writing, I work across the content ecosystem, from Google Search Console audits and on-page SEO to Facebook Ads copy and Canva creatives. I think about content as a system, not a series of isolated articles.

Case Study 01

Growing Organic Traffic for a B2B SaaS Blog (iNymbus)

+

The Problem

iNymbus had a technically strong product in accounts receivable automation, but their blog was not capturing search traffic for the specific terms their target buyers, CPG suppliers, and AR managers, were actually searching for. Content existed, but wasn't structured for search intent or internal linking.

Approach & Strategy

  • Conducted full keyword gap analysis using Ahrefs against the top 3 ranking competitors
  • Mapped content topics to buyer journey stages (awareness → consideration → decision)
  • Created topic clusters around "deduction management," "DSO," and "accounts receivable automation"
  • Optimized existing pages for heading structure, internal linking, and featured snippet capture
  • Wrote 25+ long-form pieces (1,500–3,000 words) with embedded structured data

Results

Across the content published:

↑ 42% organic sessions (6 months) 8 keywords on Page 1 3 featured snippets captured ↓ 18% bounce rate

Case Study 03

Retail Content Strategy for Mobile-First Audience (Smart Webtech)

+

The Problem

A retail technology client needed content that spoke to retail business owners, a non-technical audience, about complex topics like app development costs and ROI. Previous content was too technical and had poor engagement metrics.

Approach & Strategy

  • Rewrote buyer personas and matched content tone accordingly
  • Used Microsoft Clarity heatmaps to understand scroll depth issues on existing pages
  • Restructured articles with clear H2 sections, comparison tables, and summary boxes
  • Added FAQ schema markup for PAA visibility
  • Integrated social distribution plan (LinkedIn + Facebook Ads)

Results

↑ 35% avg. time on page PAA box earned (2 articles) ↑ 28% CTR from SERP ↓ 22% exit rate
